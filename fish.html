Welcome to the FISH Pond!

Introduction

Festival Instruction, Support, and Humor (affectionately known as FISH) is a Southern California based Renaissance Faire Guild with the goal of introducing beginning Faire Folk to the concepts and rules of being part of Renaissance Faire. We provide a safe and welcoming environment to learn the ropes, develop Faire personae, and find your niche.

How do we present this idea at Faire?

We are a large extended family, involved in every business, legitimate and otherwise. Our goals: learn all that can be learned, do all that can be done, celebrate all that can be celebrated. …to Follow the Money, and Don’t Get Caught… We are builders of Shtick. We dress in honor of those who have come before us. We are providers of Festival Instruction, Support, and Humor. Who are you with? I'm with FISH!

Come join us and get hooked!

What is Renaissance Faire?

Renaissance faire is a slice of the past, brought forward, for our education and enjoyment. Guild members, performers, and vendors gather several times a year to join with the public and celebrate the finer parts of the 15th century.

What do Guild Members do?

While the guests are only on site during open hours (usually 10am to 6pm or so), those working the Faire camp on site for the duration of the Faire. It is our job to provide atmosphere and interaction for the Faire visitors.

Our Public Face

As members of the Faire, guild members have certain responsibilities. Each guild participates in Gate Duty, where the guild gathers at the front gates to set the stage for the visitors and get them in the mood for the festivities that await. This may include talking with visitors, dancing, singing, jesting, or playing musical instruments as fits with your chosen shtick.

There is also a daily parade around the faire grounds lead by the Queen and her entourage. All guilds not currently at gate duty are requested to join in the processional.

Some guild yards are designated as checkpoints for the Queen’s Quest where young patrons can learn about the era and practice skills taught at each station. Each checkpoint signs off on the quest form allowing the patrons to return to the Queen and be knighted for their efforts. Our personal checkpoint teaches the patrons about the art of barter.

Some faire folk decide that performing is their shtick of choice. Our guild is associated with a band by the name of Cutthroat Reef that performs several times a day, each day of faire.

So whats a Shtick?

A shtick is not something issued upon becoming a member to beat the patrons with! A shtick is a persona, honed over time, to show our best face to the public.

Your shtick is what allows you to leave work Friday afternoon “Joe, the Investment Banker” and display your true self, “Westlan, Knight of the Realm!”, for the weekend at least! Each shtick is unique and adds flair to the environment. Do you wish to be a merchant from a far off land, come to peddle your wares? Would you prefer to be a plague doctor, or a baker, or a scribe?

One of the purposes of our guild is to help you build your shtick from the ground up, from name to occupation, to accent and garb. If you don’t already know what your want or how to do it, THAT’S OK. That’s what we are here for, and Master Fish will be happy to teach you the rules.

How to Join

Joining the guild is easy!

Master Fish is the head of our house. If you are interested in joining our guild there is some simple paperwork to fill out and submit to him so we can partner you up with an Aunt or Uncle to get you integrated into the guild. While there is a lot of work that goes into putting our best foot forward to the public and keeping the camp running smoothly, it balances nicely with the fun and friends you will have with us.

There are a few things you will need to get started!

The Escondido Renaissance Faire spans two weekends, twice a year, in the Spring and Fall. As a guild member you will be expected to camp on site each weekend if at all possible. This requires basic camping gear, and you will definitely want a chair for use during the day. A list of suggested camping gear can be found here.
